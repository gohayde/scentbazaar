Frequently Asked Questions

How much fragrance oil can I add to your lotion base?

Our premium unscented lotion bases are formulated to remain stable while carrying a high fragrance load. Depending on the specific oil and its composition, you can generally blend between 2% and 5% fragrance oil by weight without causing emulsion breakdown, greasiness, or separation. Lighter, high-alcohol fragrance oils typically blend cleanly at the higher end of this range, while heavier oudh and resinous oils may require a small solubiliser to fully incorporate at concentrations above 3%. We recommend always conducting a small-batch trial at your target percentage — mix 100g of base with your chosen oil, allow it to rest for 48 hours at room temperature, and inspect for separation or texture changes before scaling up. All fragrance oils supplied by Scent Bazaar from our 2,000-plus oil catalogue come with MSDS and COA documentation to support your formulation records and any regulatory submissions.

They are formulated without animal-derived ingredients and are produced cruelty-free, making them suitable for brands with vegan or ethical sourcing commitments. Each base is supplied with a full technical data sheet covering INCI ingredient listing, pH range, recommended usage levels, and shelf life — the information your regulatory team or contract manufacturer will need to complete a product safety assessment before launching commercially in the UAE, EU, UK, or other regulated markets. Can I mix different cosmetic bases together to create a hybrid product?

Yes, within limits. Many of our bases are designed to be chemically compatible with each other — for example, blending our lotion base with body oil base at a 70:30 ratio can create a luxurious lightweight body milk with a dry-touch skin feel that neither base achieves alone. Similarly, mixing shower gel base with a small proportion of conditioning oil base produces a moisturising wash formula popular in the GCC market. However, not all combinations will remain stable — water-based and oil-based formulas require an emulsifier to bind properly, and some combinations will separate on standing if the ratios or emulsification chemistry is incorrect. Always test small batches of 100–200g first, observe the result over 48 to 72 hours at room temperature, then conduct a heat-cool cycling test before scaling to production volumes. Our team is available via WhatsApp to advise on compatible base combinations for your specific product concept.

Are your cosmetic bases suitable for the hot Middle Eastern climate?

Yes. All our cosmetic bases are specifically formulated and stability-tested for hot, dry climates typical of the UAE and wider GCC region. Standard cosmetic bases formulated for temperate European or North American markets frequently experience emulsion breakdown, phase separation, or melting at the sustained high temperatures common in the Gulf — particularly during summer months when ambient temperatures can exceed 45°C and retail or storage environments may not be consistently air-conditioned. Our bases maintain their viscosity, texture, and emulsion stability even when exposed to temperatures up to 40°C and low humidity conditions, ensuring your lotions, serums, and body care products perform consistently throughout the year. They are also tested for resistance to thermal cycling — the repeated heating and cooling that occurs during daytime shipping and overnight storage — which is one of the most common causes of early product failure in this region.

Do you supply bases suitable for making solid perfume?

Yes. Our solid perfume base is a carefully balanced blend of cosmetic-grade waxes and skin-conditioning butters designed to carry fragrance oils in a portable, finger-application format that requires no bottle, pump, or alcohol. The base sets to a firm but pliable consistency at room temperature that melts slightly on contact with warm skin, releasing the fragrance oil it carries and delivering it effectively to pulse points. It is paraben-free, skin-safe, and compatible with both synthetic and natural fragrance oils at fragrance loads of up to 15% by weight — significantly higher than most lotion or spray formats. This high load capacity makes solid perfume particularly well-suited to concentrating richly aromatic oud, musk, and resinous Arabic fragrance profiles. Solid perfume bases are supplied in 1kg and 5kg quantities and pair directly with fragrance oils from our 2,000-plus oil catalogue, with a 100ml minimum per oil.

What is the difference between a perfume base and a fragrance fixative?

A perfume base is a carrier formulation — lotion, wax, alcohol, or oil — used to dilute and physically deliver the fragrance oil to the user's skin or environment. It determines the product format: a lotion base produces a scented body lotion, a wax base produces a solid perfume, and perfumers alcohol produces an eau de parfum or cologne spray. A fixative, by contrast, is a specific ingredient added within any of these carriers to chemically slow the evaporation rate of the volatile top-note compounds in the fragrance oil, extending how long the scent remains perceptible on skin. Common fixatives include benzyl benzoate, iso E Super, and various musk aroma chemicals. Our perfumers alcohol already contains carefully dosed fixative compounds, and our lotion and wax bases are formulated to retain fragrance character through their own film-forming properties, meaning additional fixatives are not required in most standard applications.
