The Art of Fumigation

How Is Authentic Bakhoor and Dukhoon Made?

In Middle Eastern culture, scenting the home and clothing with smoldering incense is a sign of ultimate hospitality and prestige. As a deeply rooted Bakhoor supplier in Dubai, Scent Bazaar masterfully blends traditional techniques with modern, consistent manufacturing protocols to deliver premium incense to global retailers.

We supply two primary categories of traditional incense. Dukhoon is created by grinding premium Oud wood and sandalwood into a fine powder, mixing it with binding resins, sugar, and heavy arabic fragrance oils, and pressing it into tablets. Oud Ma'attar involves taking pure, raw agarwood chips and soaking them deep in concentrated attars for months to absorb the scent.

Our Deira factory produces both styles in massive wholesale quantities, ensuring perfectly balanced moisture content so the incense smolders slowly on charcoal rather than burning up instantly.

What is the difference between Bakhoor, Dukhoon, and pure Oud?

Pure Oud refers to unadulterated agarwood chips that are burned directly for their raw, resinous natural scent — nothing is added. Bakhoor is the broader term for scented woodchips, most commonly called Oud Ma'attar, where natural agarwood or a carrier wood is soaked or infused with fragrance oils, musks, and resins to create a layered, blended smoke profile. The oil infusion allows manufacturers to introduce modern or custom scent profiles into the incense. Dukhoon is a distinct format: fine wood powder mixed with fragrance oils and binding agents, then pressed into solid tablets, balls, or cones. Each format burns differently — pure Oud gives a deep, complex smoke over a long period, Bakhoor delivers a rich blended fragrance, while Dukhoon burns cleanly and consistently with a more uniform fragrance release. Are there customs restrictions on shipping Oud wood internationally?

Real agarwood (Aquilaria species) is listed under CITES Appendix II, which means all international trade in genuine agarwood requires valid export permits and documentation confirming the wood was legally sourced and that the trade does not threaten the species. As experienced exporters shipping to 50+ countries, Scent Bazaar manages the necessary CITES certifications for genuine agarwood shipments, ensuring all paperwork is correct and compliant before despatch. For buyers where CITES documentation creates delays or import complications in their destination market, we can formulate high-fidelity synthetic Oud alternatives using agarwood-derived aroma chemicals that replicate the smoke profile of natural oud without the regulatory burden. These alternatives perform comparably for most Bakhoor applications and clear customs without issue. We advise all international buyers to verify their country's specific import requirements for natural resins and wood products before placing an order, and our team can help clarify exactly what documentation will be needed.

How long should quality Bakhoor burn on charcoal?

Quality Bakhoor should burn slowly and consistently for 20 to 40 minutes per piece on properly heated charcoal. Burn time is determined by two main factors: the density of the woodchip substrate and the fragrance oil load applied during manufacture. Heavily resin-saturated chips with a high oil load will burn longer and release more fragrance, while lighter or thinner chips will combust faster and produce a shorter, lighter effect. Our premium Oud Ma'attar products are formulated by our chemical engineering team to achieve a controlled, slow burn without producing excessive smoke or an acrid edge — a common problem with poorly formulated Bakhoor that uses cheap filler wood or synthetic carriers that combust harshly. The fragrance released from a single piece will typically linger in a medium-sized room for two to four hours after the chip has finished burning, as the scent molecules deposit on soft furnishings, fabric, and walls.

Can Bakhoor be used in an electric incense burner instead of charcoal?

Yes. Our Bakhoor and Dukhoon products are compatible with both traditional charcoal Mabkhara burners and modern electric incense heaters. Electric burners work by heating a ceramic or metal plate to a controlled temperature, typically between 200°C and 400°C, which gently vaporises the fragrance oils in the Bakhoor without full combustion. This produces a smoke-free or very low-smoke fragrance experience — ideal for hotel lobbies, spa treatment rooms, corporate offices, shopping mall environments, and any indoor setting where open-fire charcoal burning is not permitted under fire safety regulations or is simply impractical to manage. The fragrance character produced by electric heating is slightly softer and more sustained compared with charcoal burning: the intense, variable heat of glowing coal produces a more assertive and complex smoke with a richer depth, while electric heat gives a gentler, more even release. We stock both traditional and electric burner formats at wholesale quantities for hospitality procurement and retail buyers.

How is Bakhoor used correctly to get the best scent performance?

For best results, heat your Mabkhara burner with quality charcoal — allow the disc to fully ignite and glow orange before use, which typically takes three to five minutes. Once the coal is ready, place one piece of Bakhoor directly onto the glowing surface. Do not hold it over an open flame, as direct fire combusts the wood too quickly and produces harsh smoke rather than the layered fragrance profile the product is formulated for. The sustained heat of the coal vaporises the fragrance oils gradually and cleanly. Hold the burner close to fabric — abayas, thobes, curtains, and cushions — to allow the smoke to infuse into the fibres, which is how the longest-lasting effect is achieved. Pass it around the room at shoulder height for general ambient scenting. A single piece of quality Bakhoor will fragrance a medium room for 30 to 60 minutes, with deposited scent lingering on fabric for hours. Always place the burner on a heat-proof surface and never leave it unattended.
