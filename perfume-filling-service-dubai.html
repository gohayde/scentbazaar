The Bottling Process

From Mixing to Retail Assembly

Our filling operations are integrated seamlessly into our wider fragrance supply business. By utilizing our wholesale perfume oils and our technical-grade perfumers alcohol, we offer a completely consolidated supply chain.

  • Chilled Filtration: Before any liquid enters a bottle, our compounded fragrances undergo chilling and micro-filtration. This crucial step removes microscopic waxes and particulates, guaranteeing the final perfume is remarkably clear and stable.
  • Pneumatic Crimping: Securing the spray pump (atomizer) to the glass is the most critical point of failure in perfumery. We utilize pneumatic crimping machines that apply exact, uniform pressure to secure the collar, preventing leaks and evaporation.
  • Finishing & Cello-wrapping: After filling and crimping, our team affixes your custom labels, places the bottles in their designated retail boxes, and applies a tamper-proof cellophane wrap for that premium unboxing experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is your production capacity for perfume filling?

Our automated lines are capable of high-volume output. We comfortably manage daily runs spanning from small 500-unit niche batches up to multi-thousand unit mass-market orders without sacrificing quality or finish.

Can you fill perfumes into bottles I supply myself?

Yes. While we strongly recommend using our vetted perfume bottles and packaging to ensure perfect crimping compatibility, we can contract-fill into client-supplied glass, provided it passes our quality control engineering checks.

Do you filter the perfume prior to filling?

Absolutely. Maceration and deep-chill filtration are standard procedures in our filling protocol. We ensure all compounded perfumes are crystal clear and free from insoluble waxes before the filling process begins.

How do you ensure the spray pumps don't leak?

Leaking is prevented through exact pneumatic crimping. Our machines are calibrated precisely to the diameter and depth of your specific bottle neck and pump actuator, creating an airtight, factory seal.

What bottle sizes and neck finishes can your filling machines accommodate?

Our filling lines accommodate bottles ranging from 5ml to 500ml across all standard neck finishes including FEA 15, FEA 20, and custom neck sizes. We maintain a diverse library of compatible crimping heads and pump adapters, ensuring your specific bottle geometry is perfectly sealed during fill.

Can you fill roll-on bottles and non-spray formats?

Yes. Beyond spray atomizers, we accommodate roll-on applicators (roll-ball bottles), liquid pump bottles, and cap-sealed formats. Each format requires specific machine calibration and handling protocols that our team has perfected through years of contract manufacturing.

What is the minimum batch size for your perfume filling service?

We accept batches from 500 units upwards for niche and indie brands, with no requirement to commit to large industrial runs from the outset. This makes our filling service accessible for brand launches, limited editions, and seasonal collections. For clients scaling production, our automated lines can manage 50,000+ units per run efficiently.

Do you handle labelling and outer packaging as part of the filling service?

Yes. Our filling service is fully integrated. Beyond filling and crimping, we handle label application, cello-wrapping, and boxing into presentation cartons. We supply matching outer packaging — rigid boxes, carton sleeves, and custom inserts — so your product comes out shelf-ready and export-ready from a single facility.

Do you provide fragrance maceration before filling?

Yes. All fragrance compounds are macerated and cold-filtered before filling begins. Maceration allows the fragrance molecules to fully integrate with the alcohol carrier, improving scent balance, longevity, and clarity. Deep-chill filtration removes any wax particulates that would otherwise cause cloudiness in the final bottled product.

Can you source the fragrance oil and fill it in one order?

Yes — and this is one of our key advantages. As a vertically integrated fragrance supplier, we can select or formulate the fragrance oil, dilute it with perfumers alcohol, mature the blend, fill it into bottles, crimp, label, and package — all in one place. This eliminates coordination between multiple suppliers and reduces your lead time significantly.

What quality checks are performed after filling?

Each filled unit undergoes visual clarity inspection (no cloudiness or particulates), leak testing on the crimp seal, and actuator function testing to confirm spray pattern and dosage. Batches are also checked for fill volume accuracy. Any unit failing quality control is removed before packing. Full batch records are maintained for traceability.
