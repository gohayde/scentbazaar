The Bottling Process

What Happens at Each Stage of the Filling Process?

Our filling operations are integrated seamlessly into our wider fragrance supply business. By utilizing our wholesale perfume oils and our technical-grade perfumers alcohol, we offer a completely consolidated supply chain.

  • Chilled Filtration: Before any liquid enters a bottle, our compounded fragrances undergo chilling and micro-filtration. This crucial step removes microscopic waxes and particulates, guaranteeing the final perfume is remarkably clear and stable.
  • Pneumatic Crimping: Securing the spray pump (atomizer) to the glass is the most critical point of failure in perfumery. We utilize pneumatic crimping machines that apply exact, uniform pressure to secure the collar, preventing leaks and evaporation.
  • Finishing & Cello-wrapping: After filling and crimping, our team affixes your custom labels, places the bottles in their designated retail boxes, and applies a tamper-proof cellophane wrap for that premium unboxing experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is your production capacity for perfume filling?

Our automated filling lines at our Dubai facility are engineered for both boutique and high-volume production runs. We comfortably process batches starting from 500 units for niche and indie brands, scaling through to tens of thousands of units per run for mass-market clients. Each line operates with precision-controlled filling heads that maintain consistent fill volumes throughout the batch, whether we are running 50ml eau de parfum bottles or 100ml flacons. Our team manages multiple concurrent production orders without cross-contamination, using dedicated line changeover protocols between fragrance batches. Turn-around times depend on batch size, bottle format, and whether maceration is required before filling, but we maintain realistic scheduling that avoids rushed production and the quality failures that come with it.

Can you fill perfumes into bottles I supply myself?

Yes. While we strongly recommend using our vetted perfume bottles and packaging to ensure perfect crimping compatibility, we can contract-fill into client-supplied glass, provided it passes our quality control engineering checks. When you supply your own bottles, our team inspects each piece for neck diameter consistency, glass wall integrity, and pump fitment before committing to a run. Inconsistent neck tolerances in client-supplied glass are the most common cause of leaking or damaged crimps, which is why pre-production QC is non-negotiable. We recommend sending a sample of your bottles in advance so we can calibrate our crimping heads precisely. Clients supplying their own packaging remain responsible for bottle quality; any units rejected during our incoming inspection are set aside and not filled.

Do you filter the perfume prior to filling?

Absolutely. Maceration and deep-chill filtration are both standard procedures in our pre-filling protocol. After the fragrance oil is blended with perfumers alcohol, the compound is left to macerate — allowing the aromatic molecules to fully integrate with the carrier — before being chilled to between 0°C and -5°C. This cold treatment causes any insoluble waxes and natural resins to crystallise and separate, after which we filter the blend through a series of progressively finer filter papers. The result is a crystal-clear, polished liquid free from haziness or particulates that would otherwise be visible through glass bottles. This process is particularly critical for oud-heavy or natural-rich formulas, which contain higher levels of wax-bearing materials than synthetic accords. We never skip this step, regardless of batch size.

How do you ensure the spray pumps don't leak?

Leaking is prevented primarily through exact pneumatic crimping, which is the most critical mechanical step in the entire filling process. Our crimping machines are individually calibrated to the precise diameter, depth, and wall thickness of your specific bottle neck and pump actuator collar before any production run begins. We conduct test crimps on sample units first, checking the gasket compression visually and by applying a vacuum-pressure leak test. Only once the test crimps pass do we proceed with full production. Beyond crimping, we also verify that the pump dip tube length is correctly matched to the bottle height so the pump draws liquid cleanly without air locks. Post-filling, each unit undergoes a manual inversion leak check before being packed into cartons, providing a final safeguard against any defective seals.

What bottle sizes and neck finishes can your filling machines accommodate?

Our filling lines accommodate bottles ranging from 5ml miniatures up to 500ml statement flacons, covering all standard fragrance neck finishes including FEA 15 and FEA 20 — the two most common crimping standards used globally in fine fragrance. We maintain a comprehensive library of compatible crimping heads, pump adapters, and nozzle assemblies, so the majority of commercially available bottle geometries can be handled without requiring custom tooling. For non-standard or proprietary bottle necks, we request a sample in advance to fabricate or source the correct tooling prior to your production date. If you are sourcing bottles through us, our procurement team will ensure the bottles specified are confirmed compatible with our lines before your order is placed, preventing any compatibility issues from arising on production day.

Can you fill roll-on bottles and non-spray formats?

Yes. Beyond spray atomizers, we accommodate roll-on applicators (roll-ball bottles), liquid pump bottles, reed diffuser bottles, and cap-sealed formats such as traditional attar bottles. Roll-on formats are particularly popular for concentrated perfume oils and oud-based attars, where direct skin application is preferred over misting. Each format requires specific machine calibration and handling protocols: roll-on filling uses different nozzle assemblies and capping pressure compared to crimped spray formats, and some formats require manual capping for small batches where automated handling would risk breakage. Our team has refined procedures for all common non-spray formats through extensive contract manufacturing experience. If you have an unusual applicator format in mind, send us a sample and we will advise on feasibility before you commit to a production order.

Do you handle labelling and outer packaging as part of the filling service?

Yes. Our filling service is fully integrated from liquid preparation through to export-ready finished product. Beyond filling and crimping, we handle pressure-sensitive label application to the bottle body and cap, cello-wrapping for tamper evidence and presentation, and boxing into your chosen outer carton. Clients provide their artwork files and approved samples, and we handle everything through to palletised, export-ready dispatch from our Dubai warehouse.

Do you provide fragrance maceration before filling?

Yes. All fragrance compounds prepared for filling undergo a proper maceration period before the filling process begins. Maceration is the resting phase during which the fragrance oil fully dissolves and integrates with the perfumers alcohol carrier, allowing top, heart, and base notes to harmonise and the full aromatic character of the blend to develop. Skipping maceration often results in a flat, harsh opening note on the final product. Our standard maceration period is a minimum of 48 to 72 hours for most accords, with heavier oud-based or resinous formulas rested longer. Following maceration, the blend undergoes deep-chill filtration to remove wax particulates before being pumped directly into our filling lines. The combined effect is a clear, polished, well-rounded final product that performs correctly from the first spray.

Can you source the fragrance oil and fill it in one order?

Yes — and this is one of our most significant operational advantages as a vertically integrated fragrance supplier. From our Souq Naif, Deira base, we can select from over 2,000 in-stock fragrance oils or develop a custom formula through our blending team, dilute it to your target concentration with perfumers alcohol, macerate and filter the compound, fill it into your chosen bottles, crimp and label, and pack into export cartons — all in one facility under a single purchase order. Clients who source fragrance oil from one supplier, alcohol from another, and filling from a third routinely encounter coordination delays when any single element is late or inconsistent. Our end-to-end model eliminates that risk and gives you a single point of accountability from raw material through to finished, shelf-ready product.

What quality checks are performed after filling?

Each filled unit passes through a structured quality control sequence before being released for packing. Visual clarity inspection confirms no cloudiness, haze, or visible particulates are present in the liquid. Crimp seal integrity is checked on a sample basis using inversion and pressure testing to confirm no leakage around the pump gasket. Actuator function testing verifies correct spray pattern, dosage per actuation, and that the pump primes within an acceptable number of strokes. Fill volume accuracy is monitored throughout the run using periodic weight checks against the target fill weight for your specific liquid density and bottle size. Any units failing these checks are quarantined and not packed. Full batch production records — including fill date, fragrance batch code, and QC results — are retained for traceability and are available to clients on request.
