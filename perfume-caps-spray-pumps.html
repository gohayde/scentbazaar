Frequently Asked Questions

What is an FEA 15 crimp neck?

FEA 15 is the internationally recognised standard neck diameter of 15mm for luxury fine fragrance glass bottles, defined by the Fragrance Foundation of Europe and adopted as the default by the vast majority of global perfume manufacturers. The designation ensures that spray pumps, collars, and crimping tooling from different suppliers are interchangeable across compliant bottles. The vast majority of our premium crimp-on atomizers, decorative collars, and Zamac caps are designed and dimensionally tested to lock perfectly onto an FEA 15mm neck, providing a leak-proof, rattle-free fit. When sourcing bottles and hardware separately — for example, buying glass bottles from one supplier and pumps from us — always confirm that your bottle is FEA 15 compliant before ordering atomizers. We supply FEA 15 hardware to fragrance brands, private label manufacturers, and perfume filling operations across more than 50 countries worldwide.

What is a 'collar' in perfume packaging?

Once an atomizer is crimped onto the glass bottle neck, the process of bending the metal skirt tightly under the glass rim leaves the crimped area looking rough and industrial — not acceptable for a premium retail product. A collar is a smooth, decorative metal ring, typically manufactured from Zamac or plated steel, that slides down over the crimped pump base to conceal the mechanics completely and provide a clean, polished visual transition between the bottle and the spray pump stem. It also acts as the structural seat on which the outer perfume cap rests when closed, ensuring the cap sits level and flush without wobbling. Collars are available in the same finishes as our caps — gold, silver, matte black, and rose gold — and should always be ordered in the same finish to ensure a cohesive, matched hardware presentation across the completed bottle assembly.

Do I need special machines to use crimp-on atomizers?

Yes, unlike screw-on tops, crimp atomizers require a controlled mechanical force to bend the metal skirt tightly and evenly under the glass rim to create a secure, leak-proof seal. This cannot be done reliably by hand and attempting to do so risks cracking the glass neck or producing an uneven crimp that allows fragrance to leak during shipping. For startup brands and small-batch producers, we sell manual hand-crimping tools that are affordable, require no power supply, and produce a clean consistent crimp on FEA 15 and FEA 20 bottles when used correctly. For higher-volume operations — typically above 500 units per batch — a benchtop or floor-standing pneumatic crimping machine is recommended to maintain speed, consistency, and operator safety. Alternatively, for brands that prefer not to invest in equipment at all, we can handle the entire filling, crimping, collar fitting, capping, and outer boxing process via our contract filling service, delivering finished retail-ready product directly to you.

Do you supply Zamac caps for roll-on (rollerball) perfume bottles?

Yes. In addition to spray atomizers, we stock premium Zamac (zinc alloy) roller ball tops and outer caps specifically designed for roll-on applicator bottles used with concentrated attar oils and oil-based fragrances. Roll-on closures serve a different function from spray caps — the Zamac outer cap must provide a firm, leak-proof press-fit over the stainless steel roller ball assembly to prevent oil seeping during storage and transit, particularly important given the high viscosity and staining potential of concentrated oud and musk oils. Our Zamac roll-on caps are dimensionally matched to standard glass and metal roll-on bottle necks in 3ml, 6ml, 10ml, and 12ml formats, and are available in chrome, gold, matte black, and custom electroplate finishes to match your branding. Roll-on packaging is increasingly popular globally, and is the dominant format among Arabic and Middle Eastern fragrance brands where concentrated oil-based attars applied directly to skin represent the traditional and preferred premium fragrance experience.

How do I match the correct pump size to my perfume bottle?

The pump size must match your bottle's neck diameter exactly. Most premium perfume bottles manufactured to international standards use either FEA 15 (15mm) or FEA 20 (20mm) neck diameters — FEA 15 is standard for fine fragrance bottles from 30ml to 100ml, while FEA 20 is common in larger body mist and personal care formats. Beyond the neck diameter, the correct dip tube length is also critical: a dip tube that is too short will leave fragrance inaccessible at the base of the bottle, while one that is too long will kink and block the pump mechanism. We provide full sizing guidance and technical spec sheets for all our atomizers, including dip tube lengths matched to our standard bottle heights. If you are sourcing bottles from a third-party supplier, share the bottle's dimensions with us and we will advise on the correct pump specification before you order.

What cap materials do you supply for perfume bottles?

We supply caps in four primary materials, each suited to a different brand aesthetic and price point. Zamac (zinc alloy) caps provide premium weight and a luxurious hand-feel in gold, silver, matte black, and rose gold electroplate finishes — the standard choice for mid-range to luxury fragrance lines. Surlyn resin caps offer high optical clarity, making them popular for minimalist and niche brands where the pump mechanism or liquid inside the bottle is intended to be seen. Natural wood caps convey warmth and organic authenticity, making them ideal for botanical, wellness, and artisan perfume brands. Magnetic-closure Zamac caps deliver a satisfying tactile click when placed on the bottle and are favoured on premium collector or gift-edition launches. All four cap types are available in FEA 15 and FEA 20 formats to match your chosen bottle standard.

Are your spray pumps compatible with high-concentration Extrait de Parfum?

Yes. Our high-dosage European-style crimp-on atomizers are specifically engineered to handle fragrance concentrations up to 30% oil by volume, making them fully suitable for Extrait de Parfum, pure attar spray, and highly concentrated fragrance mist formats. This matters because standard spray pump mechanisms — designed for Eau de Toilette or body spray concentrations of 5–10% — frequently clog when used with high-oil formulas. The viscous oil coats the internal pump valve and dip tube, reducing actuation force and eventually blocking the mechanism entirely, which causes returns and customer complaints. Our high-dosage atomizers use wider internal tolerances, heavier spring tension, and chemically resistant internal materials to ensure consistent, full-mist actuation with every press even after prolonged use at high oil concentrations. They are suitable for use with all fragrance oils from our 2,000-plus oil catalogue, and are stocked ready in Dubai for prompt dispatch.
