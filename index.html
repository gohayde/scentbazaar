What is the difference between Eau de Parfum and Eau de Toilette?

Eau de Parfum (EDP) contains 15–20% fragrance oil concentrate dissolved in perfumers alcohol and typically projects for 6–8 hours on skin. Eau de Toilette (EDT) contains 5–15% oil and lasts 3–6 hours, making it lighter and more suitable for warmer climates or daytime wear. Extrait de Parfum carries 25–40% oil concentration and can last 12 hours or more, though it is more expensive to produce. For oil-based attars, no alcohol is needed — the concentrated oil is applied directly to pulse points and can persist for 8–12 hours, which is why they remain popular in Middle Eastern perfumery. We supply both the raw concentrated oils and our technical-grade perfumers alcohol, so you have everything needed to produce any of these formats in-house at any scale.

Can I mix French fragrance oils with Arabic oud?

Yes, absolutely — and some of the most commercially successful modern fragrances are built on exactly this combination. Oud's deep, resinous, woody character anchors and extends the longevity of lighter French accords, while the French notes add lift, complexity, and broader market appeal. Floral, fruity, and spicy French oils pair particularly well with our oud, amber, saffron, and musk bases. The key is blend ratio: typically oud constitutes 10–30% of the total oil, depending on how prominent you want the Arabic character to be. Overpowering the blend with oud will limit your audience in Western markets, while too little loses the distinctiveness that differentiates an oriental fragrance. Our perfumers at the Deira store can advise on specific ratios and walk you through our hybrid blend examples during a live consultation.

What certifications do I need to export fragrance oils from Dubai?

For international export of fragrance oils you need, at minimum, a Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) and a Certificate of Analysis (COA) from your supplier — both of which we provide as standard with every order. Many markets also require allergen declarations, particularly for finished cosmetic products sold within the EU. Fragrance oils and perfumers alcohol are typically classified as flammable liquids under hazardous goods regulations, requiring Dangerous Goods (DG) documentation for air and sea freight, which our logistics team prepares for all applicable shipments. For markets with specific import controls — some African and South Asian countries have their own requirements — we can advise on destination-specific documentation before you order. Supplying the right paperwork upfront avoids delays at customs, which is why we treat documentation as a non-negotiable part of every export order.

How long does homemade or brand perfume last after mixing with alcohol?

After combining fragrance oil with perfumers alcohol, the blend should macerate for a minimum of 48–72 hours before use — this allows the alcohol and oil molecules to fully integrate, improving both scent clarity and longevity. Some complex oriental or oud-heavy blends benefit from up to two weeks of maceration. Once properly aged, a well-formulated EDP at 15–20% oil concentration will typically last 6–8 hours on skin, while an Extrait de Parfum at 25% or higher can last 12 hours or more. Wear time also depends on skin type, application point, and climate — dry skin and heat both accelerate fade. Shelf life of the finished bottled product is typically 18–24 months when stored away from heat, direct sunlight, and humidity. Storing bottles in a cool, dark location preserves both the scent profile and commercial quality.
