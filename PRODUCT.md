# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three distinct buyer types, all served simultaneously:

1. **Perfumers launching brands** — entrepreneurs building a perfume line from scratch, selecting oils, needing private label manufacturing and packaging. Sophisticated taste, design-sensitive, want to feel they're working with a credible expert, not a commodity supplier.

2. **Retailers and souk traders** — established merchants in GCC and wider Middle East buying wholesale in quantity. Price-aware but quality-driven, trust-based relationships matter. Often repeat buyers.

3. **International importers** — procurement managers and distributors in Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia, Europe buying in bulk. Need documentation (MSDS, COA), compliance assurance, reliable logistics. Decision is professional and export-focused.

All three land on the same homepage. The site must speak to the expert buyer, not the casual browser.

## Product Purpose

Scent Bazaar is a B2B wholesale fragrance supplier operating from Souq Naif, Deira, Dubai. Founded by chemical engineer Nawar Mohammed. Stocks 2,000+ concentrated perfume oils (French designer-inspired accords, authentic Arabic oud and mukhallat, single-note aroma chemicals). Services: wholesale supply, private label manufacturing (3–11 week turnaround), custom blending, contract filling and crimping, global export to 50+ countries. Physical showroom rated 4.9/5 from 162+ verified reviews. MOQ 100ml.

Success looks like a wholesale buyer landing on the homepage and immediately understanding: this is a serious, expert-level supplier, not a market stall. They inquire via WhatsApp or visit the store.

## Brand Personality

**Expert. Authoritative. Warm.**

The business is built on a chemical engineer's precision and decades of fragrance trade heritage in Deira, but it is not cold or corporate. It carries the warmth of Dubai's old souq culture alongside professional B2B confidence. Think: a master perfumer who happens to also run a global export operation. Knowledgeable, unhurried, quietly proud.

The site should feel like walking into a very well-appointed fragrance house in a heritage district — not a SaaS landing page, not an e-commerce store, not a generic "luxury" template.

## Anti-references

- Generic fragrance e-commerce stores (Fragrantica aesthetic — crowded, review-focused)
- SaaS "hero metric" templates (big number, stat row, gradient accent — the exact pattern to avoid)
- Overcrowded wholesale supplier directories (Alibaba-adjacent design)
- Identikit "luxury brand" templates with glassmorphism, dark mode purple gradients, and floating sparkles
- Anything that reads as AI-generated on first glance

## Design Principles

1. **Expert confidence over decoration** — every design decision should communicate mastery. Restraint is more authoritative than ornamentation. Cut anything that exists to fill space.

2. **Copy that earns its place** — this site has extraordinary depth of knowledge. Every section should demonstrate that depth through specific, substantive writing, not generic "premium quality" filler.

3. **The supplier you trust before you meet them** — buyers will whatsapp before visiting. The site is the first handshake. It should feel trustworthy, competent, and worth a flight to Dubai.

4. **Arabic and French, both at home** — the dual heritage of French precision and Arabic tradition is the product's unique position. Design and copy should reflect both, never defaulting to one or the other.

5. **Earn the scroll** — each section should give the buyer something new: a reason to stay, a capability they hadn't considered, a level of specificity that builds confidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Sufficient contrast on all text, especially on parchment and dark backgrounds
- All interactive elements keyboard-accessible
- Reduced motion support for animations
- All images have meaningful alt text
