How Did Scent Bazaar Start?

I started Scent Bazaar in 2025 because the fragrance supply market had a problem I kept running into: you could find cheap oils or you could find quality oils, but finding someone who actually understood what they were selling — the chemistry, the compliance, the real-world performance — was rare.

I'm a chemical engineer by training. Before launching Scent Bazaar, I spent years studying how fragrance molecules behave — how they interact with skin, how they perform in alcohol versus oil, how longevity and sillage are shaped by the actual composition of a blend, not just the marketing around it.

Dubai made sense as the base. Specifically Deira — the Al Sabkha and Murshid Bazaar area of Souq Naif has been the heartbeat of Arabic perfumery for generations. Oud traders, attar blenders, wholesale suppliers — all concentrated in one district. Being here is direct access to the source.

We set up at Shop No. 47, Souq Naif. People started coming in. Fragrance entrepreneurs, private label brands, perfumers stocking up on raw aroma chemicals, businesses needing custom blends. Word spread. We now ship to customers in over 50 countries and hold 100+ verified five-star Google reviews.

No investor deck, no fancy origin myth. Just a chemical engineer who understood fragrance better than most suppliers, set up in the right city, and built something honest.

Does Scent Bazaar supply compliance documentation with orders?

Yes — documentation is standard, not optional. Every order ships with a Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) and a Certificate of Analysis (COA) as a baseline. Commercial wholesale orders also include allergen declarations, which are required under EU cosmetic regulations for finished products and increasingly expected in other markets. For export orders involving flammable liquids — which includes most fragrance oils and perfumers alcohol — we prepare all required Dangerous Goods documentation for air or sea freight as applicable. Where specific import markets require additional customs certificates or regulatory paperwork, our logistics team prepares those documents in advance. We treat documentation as non-negotiable because our clients genuinely cannot import, retail, or sell their finished products without it — and delays caused by incomplete paperwork are expensive. Good documentation is part of the service.
