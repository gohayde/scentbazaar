Can you print my logo directly onto the glass bottle?

Yes. As part of our comprehensive private label service, we offer screen printing, hot foil stamping, and high-quality metallic decal application directly onto the glass hardware. Screen printing is well-suited for bold, solid-colour logos and is the most cost-effective option for medium to large runs. Hot foil stamping produces an embossed metallic finish — gold, silver, rose gold, or matte black — which is particularly popular on premium and luxury fragrance lines where the bottle itself forms a central part of the brand identity and retail shelf presence. Metallic decals allow more intricate artwork, fine line detail, and gradient colour work that screen printing cannot achieve on curved glass surfaces. All three methods are applied after bottle production and before assembly, so the branding is permanent and integral to the final packaging. Branded samples are produced for your approval before any full production run is committed, ensuring the finished result matches your design specification exactly.

Do you sell the outer cardboard packaging as well?

Yes, we supply complete turnkey packaging, including the inner rigid box, the outer carton sleeve, and the final protective cellophane wrap used in luxury retail. Our rigid inner boxes are constructed from thick, double-walled grey board lined with velvet, satin, or printed paper to suit the positioning of your brand. The outer carton sleeve can be printed with your brand colours, logo, product name, and regulatory information. Cellophane wrapping is applied as the final layer to protect packaging during shipping and to signal that the product is sealed and unused — a significant trust factor for retail buyers and end consumers alike. All three packaging layers can be sourced from us as a matched set, ensuring consistent dimensions, colour accuracy, and assembly compatibility. We supply these components to private label perfume brands exporting to over 50 countries.

Do your spray pumps require a crimping machine?

We primarily stock crimp-on atomizers because they provide the safest, most leak-proof seal for fine fragrances. Unlike screw-on pumps, a crimped atomizer is mechanically locked beneath the glass rim and cannot work loose during handling or transit — a critical requirement when shipping internationally, where packages undergo significant vibration and pressure changes. If you are manufacturing manually at smaller volumes, we offer affordable hand-crimping tools that require no electricity and produce a clean, consistent crimp without damaging the glass bottle neck when used correctly. For higher-volume production runs, you can outsource the entire filling and assembly process via our automated filling machines, where our team handles filling, crimping, collar fitting, capping, and outer boxing in a single contracted operation. This eliminates the need to invest in your own bottling equipment, which is particularly practical for growing brands that are still scaling their production output and do not yet require dedicated in-house machinery.

What is the difference between an FEA 15 and FEA 20 bottle neck?

FEA 15 and FEA 20 refer to international industry-standard bottle neck diameters of 15mm and 20mm respectively, as defined by the Fragrance Foundation of Europe and used globally in fragrance and cosmetics packaging. FEA 15 is the dominant standard for fine fragrance — the majority of premium perfume bottles from 30ml to 100ml use this neck size, and it is the format that all our standard crimp-on atomizers and Zamac caps are designed to fit. FEA 20 is used for larger-format bottles, body mists, and personal care products where a wider pump dip tube is needed to handle higher-viscosity formulas. Each standard requires its own matched crimping head, pump dip tube length, and cap inner diameter, so it is important to confirm which standard your chosen bottle uses before ordering spray pumps, collars, or caps separately.

What glass bottle sizes do you stock for perfume?

We stock a wide range of standard perfume bottle sizes: 5ml, 10ml, 15ml, 20ml, 30ml, 50ml, 100ml, and 200ml. Sizes from 30ml to 100ml are the most popular for retail perfume products, with 50ml being the global standard for mid-range fragrance launches and 100ml preferred for oud-based and concentrated Arabic perfumes where value-for-money is a key purchasing factor. The 5ml and 10ml formats are well-suited to travel sets, sample discovery kits, and gift-with-purchase promotions that allow new customers to trial a fragrance before committing to a full-size bottle. The 200ml format is popular for body mists, room sprays, and linen fragrances. All our bottles are manufactured from premium crystal-clear flint glass with polished, bubble-free walls to ensure your fragrance is displayed with maximum clarity and visual appeal. Every bottle in our range has been pre-tested for dimensional compatibility with our crimp-on atomizers, collars, and Zamac caps, so component fitment is confirmed before you order.

Do you supply roll-on (rollerball) perfume bottles?

Yes. In addition to spray-format bottles, we supply roll-on applicator bottles in both glass and metal formats. Roll-on bottles are particularly popular for concentrated oil-based attars and pure fragrance oils, where the undiluted oil is applied directly to pulse points rather than sprayed through alcohol. This applicator format has deep roots in Arabic and Middle Eastern fragrance culture and is growing in popularity globally as consumers become more interested in alcohol-free and skin-direct fragrance formats. The glass roll-on format is preferred for premium and luxury attar lines, while the stainless steel twist-top format is popular for travel-size and unisex everyday carry products. We supply matching Zamac roller ball caps and decorative closures to complete the packaging presentation, available in gold, silver, and matte black finishes. Roll-on bottles are stocked in 3ml, 6ml, 10ml, and 12ml sizes, and pair directly with our 2,000-plus oil catalogue, with a 100ml minimum per oil.
