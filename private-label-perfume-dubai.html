Our Comprehensive Process

How Does the Private Label Process Work Step by Step?

We have engineered our private label division to be as seamless and transparent as possible, catering to both niche startups requiring low MOQs and established retail chains needing high-volume production.

  1. Scent Selection & Formulation: Choose from our library of 2,000+ ready-to-use profiles or work with our lab to build a custom fragrance from raw aroma chemicals.
  2. Maceration & Maturation: We blend your chosen oils with our premium perfumers alcohol and age the mixture in chilled stainless steel vats for optimal smoothness and longevity.
  3. Packaging Selection: Select your glass bottles, premium metallic caps, atomizers, and collars directly from our extensive packaging stock.
  4. Automated Filling & Crimping: Through our perfume filling service, our automated lines accurately fill, crimp, and seal your bottles, ensuring zero leakage and a luxury retail finish.

Finally, we apply your branding, box the perfumes, and prepare the shipment with full MSDS and COA documentation, ready for international export from Dubai.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum order quantity for private label perfumes?

Our private label service is structured to work at multiple scales. For fragrance oil fills without custom packaging, the minimum is 100ml per scent — the same as our standard wholesale MOQ. For fully packaged private label units with branded labels, the typical minimum is 50 to 100 units per SKU, depending on the bottle format and label complexity. We can accommodate both startup brands launching their first product line and established retailers scaling existing ranges. If your order involves bespoke bottle moulds or embossed packaging, a higher minimum applies to offset tooling costs. Contact us via WhatsApp or visit our Souq Naif showroom in Deira to discuss your specific requirements and get a production quote.

Can you recreate an existing popular fragrance for my brand?

Yes. Our library of 2,000+ fragrance oils includes a broad range of high-fidelity, designer-inspired generic compounds that closely capture the character of well-known commercial and niche perfumes. These are not counterfeit reproductions — they are independently formulated oils that share a similar olfactive profile and are fully compliant for commercial sale under your own brand name. Our lead perfumer Nawar Mohammed, a trained chemical engineer, selects and adjusts the oil formulation to optimise longevity, projection, and skin performance for your target market. The finished product is labelled and packaged exclusively under your brand identity, with no reference to the original inspiration. This is one of the most popular entry points for new fragrance brands entering the UAE and GCC markets.

Do I need to supply my own bottles?

No, you do not. Scent Bazaar is a complete perfume bottle supplier with an extensive in-stock catalogue of premium glass bottles ranging from 5ml to 200ml in multiple shapes and finishes — clear flint, frosted, tinted, and coloured. We stock crimp-on and screw-on spray atomizers, roll-on fitments, dropper caps, Zamac collars, and magnetic rigid boxes. All bottles are pre-tested for compatibility with fragrance oils and alcohol-based perfumes, so you do not need to worry about material degradation or seal failure. If you have a proprietary bottle design, we can also work with your custom mould through our manufacturing partners. For most private label clients, our standard catalogue provides sufficient variety to create a distinctive, premium-looking product without additional tooling costs.

Do your private label products include compliance documents?

Yes. Every fragrance produced under our private label service is supplied with a full documentation package as standard. This includes a Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) detailing safe handling, storage, and transport classification, and a Certificate of Analysis (COA) confirming the fragrance composition and quality parameters. Where required for your target market, we also provide allergen declarations and Dangerous Goods (DG) shipping classification documents for air and sea freight. These documents are essential for retail distribution, import clearance, and compliance with cosmetic and consumer goods regulations in the EU, UK, GCC, and other major markets. We have extensive experience preparing documentation for over 50 destination countries, so your shipment arrives with everything customs requires.

How long does the complete private label manufacturing process take?

The timeline depends primarily on whether you are selecting from our existing library of 2,000+ fragrance oils or commissioning a bespoke custom blend. For ready-made oil selections, the process from fragrance confirmation through bottling, labelling, and quality inspection typically takes 3 to 5 weeks. For custom formulations, you should allow an additional 4 to 6 weeks for the blending, maceration, and approval stages before production begins, bringing the full timeline to 7 to 11 weeks. Label artwork and print production usually runs concurrently with blending to avoid delays. If you have a specific launch deadline, we recommend discussing your timeline at the outset so we can structure the production schedule accordingly. Rush production at reduced timelines can be accommodated for an expedited handling fee.

Do you handle label design and printing for private label products?

Yes. Our private label service is fully end-to-end, which includes label design and printing. We offer graphic design consultation to develop label artwork that reflects your brand identity — including typography, colour palette, bottle neck tags, and outer box printing. Label proofs are submitted for your approval before any print run begins, ensuring complete accuracy. We print on premium materials including matt and gloss laminate, foil-stamped stock, and transparent polypropylene labels suited to glass and frosted surfaces. All label content is reviewed for regulatory compliance in your target market — ingredient listings, safety icons, batch codes, and net volume declarations are incorporated as standard. If you supply your own completed artwork, we will check it against print specifications and proceed directly to production.

Can I private label both spray perfumes and oil-based attars?

Yes. We manufacture private label products in both formats and can combine them within the same product range under a single brand. Spray perfumes are produced by blending concentrated fragrance oil with pharmaceutical-grade perfumers alcohol at your specified concentration — Eau de Parfum, Eau de Toilette, or pure parfum — then filled and crimped into glass atomizer bottles. Oil-based attars are produced without alcohol: concentrated fragrance oil is filled directly into roller-ball, dropper, or wand applicator bottles, which is the traditional Middle Eastern format widely sold across the GCC and South Asian markets. Both formats are fully labelled, packaged, and supplied with MSDS and COA documentation. Many of our clients successfully sell both formats side-by-side under the same brand positioning.

Can you create a completely unique fragrance that no other brand will have?

Yes. Our custom blending service, led by Nawar Mohammed — a chemical engineer and trained perfumer — develops bespoke fragrance formulas built exclusively to your brief. The process begins with a detailed consultation to understand your brand identity, target market, and scent direction. Nawar then builds a proprietary formula using raw aroma chemicals and natural extracts from our ingredient library. You receive samples at each iteration for review, and the formula is refined until it meets your approval. Once signed off, the complete formulation is locked to your account in our records — we will not sell, licence, or replicate that specific formula for any other client. The intellectual property belongs entirely to your brand. This is the service of choice for luxury perfume houses, hotels, and premium retail brands requiring a genuinely exclusive signature scent.

What types of packaging are available for private label perfumes?

We offer a comprehensive range of packaging components sourced from established glass and hardware manufacturers. Glass bottle options span 5ml to 200ml in multiple shapes — rectangular, cylindrical, faceted, and rounded — in clear flint, frosted, and tinted finishes. Closure options include crimp-on fine mist atomizers, screw-on spray pumps, roll-on fitments, dropper caps, and wooden or Zamac wand applicators. Outer packaging includes rigid magnetic gift boxes, printed carton sleeves, and cellophane wrap. All components undergo compatibility testing with fragrance oil and alcohol formulations before use. If you have an existing proprietary bottle design or wish to develop a custom mould for your brand, we can facilitate this through our manufacturing partners, though custom tooling requires higher minimum volumes and a longer lead time.

Do you handle export and customs documentation for private label orders?

Yes. We manage the full documentation package required to ship private label fragrance products internationally from Dubai. This includes MSDS sheets for each fragrance, Certificates of Analysis, allergen declarations, and Dangerous Goods classification documents for perfume alcohol (UN1266) where applicable under IATA and IMDG regulations. For destinations with specific import requirements — such as EU cosmetic product notifications, GCC conformity certificates, or country-specific halal declarations — we can advise on what is needed and assist in preparing the relevant paperwork. Our logistics team has extensive experience exporting to over 50 countries across Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Americas. We recommend discussing your target market at the outset of the project so documentation requirements can be incorporated into the production timeline without causing delays at the shipping stage.

Can I order a small test batch before committing to a full private label run?

Yes, and we strongly recommend it for all new private label clients. We offer pre-production development batches — typically 50 to 100 finished units — that allow you to evaluate every aspect of the final product before committing to a full run. This includes fragrance performance on skin, longevity and projection, bottle and closure quality, label print accuracy, and overall packaging presentation. The development batch is produced under the same conditions as a full production run, so what you approve is exactly what you receive at scale. Any adjustments — to fragrance concentration, bottle selection, or label artwork — can be made before the full run begins. The cost of the development batch is credited in full against your first production order, so there is no financial penalty for taking this step.
