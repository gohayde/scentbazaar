What is a wholesale perfume oil supplier?

A wholesale perfume oil supplier is a B2B source for concentrated fragrance oils sold in bulk quantities — typically from 100ml up to drum quantities — at trade prices below retail. Unlike retail fragrance houses, wholesale suppliers provide raw fragrance materials (fragrance concentrates, attars, impression oils, oud, musks) with full safety documentation (MSDS, COA) for use in perfume manufacturing, private label production, candle making, cosmetics, and export. Are your French and Arabic oils ready for direct retail?

Our wholesale fragrance oils are 100% pure concentrated oils — not pre-diluted, not blended with carrier. What is the typical shelf life of your concentrated perfume oils?

Our concentrated perfume oils have a shelf life of 24 to 36 months when stored correctly. The key storage requirements are consistent: keep containers tightly sealed, store in a cool location between 15–20°C, away from direct sunlight, heat sources, and humidity. Exposure to heat and light are the primary causes of degradation — they accelerate oxidation in top notes and can alter the overall scent profile over time. When stored properly in their original sealed containers, most of our oils will retain their full aromatic integrity and commercial quality well beyond two years. This means it is safe to hold a reasonable level of stock without quality risk, which is important for wholesale buyers who need buffer inventory. Each batch ships with a Certificate of Analysis that includes the production date and recommended storage conditions.

What's the difference between French and Arabic fragrance oils?

French fragrance oils are designed around projection, structured fade (top, heart, and base notes), and scent profiles that have broad appeal in Western and international markets. They tend to emphasise fresh, floral, fruity, woody, and gourmand accords — styles shaped by European fine fragrance houses and consumer preferences. Arabic fragrance oils are built around depth, warmth, and complexity, with prominent and long-lasting base notes. Oud, amber, saffron, musk, and rose taifi are the pillars of traditional Arabic perfumery — rich, resinous, and designed to linger on skin and fabric for hours. Many of our wholesale clients blend both styles to create hybrid profiles that combine Western projection with Arabic depth, producing fragrances that perform well across diverse markets. We carry both categories in depth and can advise on blending approaches during a consultation.

What is the shelf life of wholesale perfume oils and how should they be stored?

Our concentrated wholesale perfume oils have a shelf life of 24–36 months under correct storage conditions. Store containers tightly sealed in a cool, dark environment between 15–20°C, away from direct sunlight, heat sources, and humidity. Heat and UV exposure are the primary causes of aromatic degradation — they accelerate oxidation in volatile top notes and can cause heavier base notes to thicken or discolour over time. Properly sealed and stored, most oils will retain full aromatic integrity and commercial quality well beyond two years, which means holding buffer stock is low-risk for wholesale buyers. We recommend storing stock in a dedicated fragrance storage area away from food, cleaning products, and any source of ignition, in line with MSDS safe storage guidance. Each batch COA includes the production date and batch reference for stock management purposes.

Can wholesale perfume oils be used directly in products, or do they need further processing?

It depends entirely on the end product format. For oil-based attars, concentrated roll-ons, and dram vials — the oil is used as supplied, with no further processing required. For spray perfumes (EDP, EDT, or Extrait de Parfum), the oil is diluted in our technical-grade perfumers alcohol at the appropriate concentration and then macerated for 48–72 hours minimum before bottling. For soy or paraffin candles, the oil is blended into the melted wax at a typical load rate of 6–10% by weight. For reed diffusers, the oil is blended with a carrier diffuser base at an appropriate ratio. For skin care and cosmetic applications, the oil is incorporated into an unscented base at a safe usage level. We can advise on the correct dilution rate and compatibility testing for each specific application — contact us before formulating if you are unsure.
